Vasopressin

A native peptide hormone central to hydration balance, vascular tone, and stress response signaling.

At a Glance

Category

Endogenous hormone / neuropeptide

Primary Roles

Water retention, blood pressure support, and pituitary signaling.

Research Lens

Physiology, critical care, and neuroendocrine regulation.

Overview

Vasopressin (antidiuretic hormone, ADH) is produced in the hypothalamus and released by the posterior pituitary. It helps regulate fluid balance by signaling the kidneys to retain water and can influence vascular tone under stress or dehydration conditions.

Research Themes

  • Fluid and electrolyte homeostasis (renal water reabsorption).
  • Vascular tone modulation in shock and critical care settings.
  • Neuroendocrine signaling linked to stress, circadian rhythm, and social behavior.

Safety & Regulatory Notes

Vasopressin is a prescription medication in clinical settings and should only be used under medical supervision. This page is for education only and does not provide dosing guidance.
