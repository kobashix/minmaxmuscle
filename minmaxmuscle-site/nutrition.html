MinMaxMuscle · Nutrition Strategy

Nutrition Built to Support Training

This nutrition framework is designed to support high-output training, preserve lean mass, and drive consistent fat loss through structured, repeatable execution. It prioritizes performance, recovery, and long-term adherence over extreme dietary restriction.

Macronutrient Structure

The program follows a 1 : 1 : 0.5 ratio of protein, carbohydrates, and fats. This structure balances performance needs with caloric control during a cutting phase.

  • Protein: Prioritized to preserve lean muscle mass during caloric restriction.
  • Carbohydrates: Included to sustain training intensity, recovery, and overall output.
  • Fats: Moderated to manage total caloric intake while maintaining satiety.

Caloric Intake

The system operates within an approximate 500-calorie daily deficit. This range is intentional and conservative, allowing fat loss to occur without significantly impairing strength, recovery, or training quality.

Aggressive deficits are avoided to reduce burnout, preserve muscle, and maintain consistency over time.

Nutrient Timing

Macronutrient distribution is adjusted throughout the day based on physiological demand rather than rigid meal rules.

  • Post-workout meals emphasize carbohydrates to support glycogen replenishment and recovery.
  • Evening meals emphasize fats to improve satiety and adherence during a caloric deficit.

Timing enhances the system but does not replace total caloric control.

Guiding Principles

  • Nutrition exists to support training, not compete with it.
  • Consistency and adherence outweigh short-term optimization.
  • Sustainable structure is favored over extreme or trend-driven diets.
